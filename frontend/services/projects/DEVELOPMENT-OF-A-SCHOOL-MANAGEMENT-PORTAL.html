Abstract

The increasing demand for efficient management of academic and administrative activities has led to the adoption of digital solutions in educational environments. This project presents the development of a School Management Portal as a model academic project designed to demonstrate the application of web-based technologies in managing school operations.

The portal simulates a centralized system for managing student records, staff information, classes, subjects, attendance, and academic results. Role-based access control is implemented to enable administrators, teachers, and students to interact with the system according to defined privileges. The system aims to improve data organization, accessibility, and administrative efficiency within a simulated school environment.

This model project serves as an educational framework for understanding system analysis, database design, and web application development. It is intended strictly for learning and demonstration purposes and is not associated with any real educational institution.

Chapter One: Introduction

1.1 Background of the Study

Educational institutions handle large volumes of academic and administrative data, including student records, staff information, class schedules, attendance, and examination results. Traditionally, many of these activities have been managed using manual or semi-automated methods such as paper records and standalone spreadsheets. While these methods may be sufficient for small-scale operations, they often become inefficient as the size and complexity of the institution increase.

The advancement of information and communication technology has introduced web-based systems that enable centralized and automated management of institutional activities. School management portals are designed to integrate various school operations into a single platform, improving data accessibility, accuracy, and administrative efficiency.

This project focuses on the development of a School Management Portal as a model academic project. The system simulates a typical school environment where administrators, teachers, and students interact with a centralized platform to manage academic and administrative processes.

This project is a model academic work developed for learning and demonstration purposes. It is not associated with any real educational institution.
